崖の上のポニョ

Ponyo on the Cliff by the Sea

A goldfish princess named Ponyo escapes from the ocean and longs to become human after falling in love with a boy named Sōsuke. Directed by Hayao Miyazaki in 2008, this Studio Ghibli masterpiece is a love letter to the sea, childhood wonder, and the magic hidden in everyday life. Ponyo's boundless energy and joy have made her one of Ghibli's most beloved characters — and it's easy to see why.

Studio Ghibli · 2008 Director: Hayao Miyazaki Magic · Ocean · Friendship

Japanese Cuisine

From street stalls to Michelin stars — Japanese food is an art form rooted in seasonality, balance, and quiet beauty.

🍣
Sushi
寿司
Vinegared rice with the freshest fish. From casual conveyor belts to 3-Michelin-star omakase experiences.
🍜
Ramen
ラーメン
Rich broths — shoyu, tonkotsu, miso, shio — slow-cooked for hours and served with noodles and toppings.
🐙
Takoyaki
たこ焼き
Osaka's iconic octopus balls — crispy outside, molten inside, topped with bonito flakes that dance in the heat.
🍡
Wagashi
和菓子
Traditional sweets crafted to mirror nature's seasons — mochi, dorayaki, yokan, nerikiri. Edible poetry.
🍵
Matcha
抹茶
Stone-ground green tea at the heart of the tea ceremony. Earthy, grassy, complex — a meditation in a bowl.
🍙
Onigiri
おにぎり
Rice triangles wrapped in nori — the perfect handheld comfort food, filled with salmon, plum, or tuna mayo.
🍤
Tempura
天ぷら
Delicate battered and fried seafood and vegetables. The lightest, crispest coating imaginable — a Portuguese-inspired Japanese art.
🌸
Sakura Sweets
桜スイーツ
Cherry blossom season brings pink everything — sakura mochi, sakura lattes, hanami dango eaten under blooming trees.

文化

Art & Living Culture

From ink on silk to contemporary manga — Japan's artistic traditions span millennia and continue to evolve.

🌊
Ukiyo-e Woodblock Prints
浮世絵
Edo-period prints capturing the "floating world" of city life, landscapes, and kabuki actors. Hokusai's Great Wave is recognized worldwide.
🌸
Ikebana
生け花
The art of flower arrangement — not decoration, but a meditative conversation between plant, space, and silence rooted in Zen philosophy.
🕊️
Origami
折り紙
The ancient art of paper folding — from cranes that grant wishes to complex mathematical sculptures pushing the boundaries of geometry.
🎭
Kabuki Theatre
歌舞伎
400 years of stylized drama — flamboyant costumes, white-painted faces, slow dramatic poses, and elaborate stagecraft in performances that last all day.
🍵
Tea Ceremony
茶道
Chado — The Way of Tea — is not about drinking tea. It is about presence, gratitude, and finding beauty in a single perfect moment of hospitality.
📚
Manga
漫画
Japan's graphic storytelling tradition spans every genre imaginable — one in four books published in Japan is manga, read by children and grandparents alike.
四季

The Four Seasons

Japan experiences each season with extraordinary depth — the cherry blossoms of spring, the festival heat of summer, autumn's fiery maples, winter's silent snow.

🌸
Spring
春 · Haru
Cherry blossoms bloom for just two weeks — the whole country gathers beneath them for hanami picnics. The pink-and-white spectacle moves north over six weeks as spring travels up the islands.
March – May · Sakura Season
🎆
Summer
夏 · Natsu
Hot, vibrant, festive. Obon lantern festivals honour ancestors, fireworks (hanabi) light up every city, and everyone wears yukata to summer matsuri. Shaved ice (kakigōri) is essential survival.
June – August · Matsuri Season
🍁
Autumn
秋 · Aki
Koyo — autumn leaf viewing — rivals spring in beauty. Kyoto's temples surrounded by crimson maples are among the most breathtaking sights in the world. Cool air, harvest foods, peaceful walks.
October – November · Koyo Season
❄️
Winter
冬 · Fuyu
Snow covers Kyoto temples and Hokkaido forests in silence. Hot springs (onsen) are at their most magical when steam rises against cold air. Winter illuminations turn cities into dreamscapes.
December – February · Snow & Onsen
